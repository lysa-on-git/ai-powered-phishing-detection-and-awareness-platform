What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cyberattack where criminals send fake emails pretending to be trusted organizations — such as banks, PayPal, or your employer — to trick you into revealing sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or personal data.

Phishing is responsible for over 90% of data breaches worldwide. Knowing how to spot it is your first line of defense.

Red Flags to Watch Out For
Urgency & Threats
Phrases like "Act now!", "Your account will be closed in 24 hours", or "Immediate action required" are designed to panic you into clicking without thinking.
Suspicious Links
Hover over links before clicking. Phishing URLs often look close to real ones: paypa1.com instead of paypal.com, or secure-bank-login.xyz.
Mismatched Sender Address
The display name may say "Amazon Support" but the actual email is something like support@amaz0n-help.ru. Always check the full address.
Too Good to Be True
Winning a prize you never entered, free gift cards, or unexpected refunds are classic phishing bait.
Unexpected Attachments
Never open attachments from unknown senders — especially .exe, .zip, or even .pdf files. They may contain malware.
Poor Grammar & Spelling
Many phishing emails contain spelling mistakes, awkward phrasing, or generic greetings like "Dear Customer" instead of your real name.
How to Stay Safe
Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
Even if attackers get your password, 2FA adds a second layer of protection they can't bypass.
Verify Before You Click
If an email asks you to log in, go directly to the website by typing the URL in your browser instead of clicking any link.
Call to Confirm
If you receive a suspicious email from your bank or a colleague, call them directly using a number you already know — not one from the email.
Keep Software Updated
Always keep your browser, antivirus, and operating system up to date to protect against known vulnerabilities.

Real Phishing Email Example
From: security@paypa1-alert.com
Subject: ⚠️ Your PayPal account has been limited

Dear Customer,

We have detected unusual activity on your account. Your account access has been temporarily limited. Please verify your informations immediately by clicking the link below:

http://paypal-secure-verify.xyz/login

Failure to verify within 24 hours will result in permanent suspension.

PayPal Security Team

What's wrong with this email?

  • Sender domain is paypa1-alert.com — not paypal.com
  • Generic greeting "Dear Customer" instead of your real name
  • Creates urgency with a 24-hour deadline
  • Grammar error: "verify your informations"
  • Link goes to a fake domain paypal-secure-verify.xyz
